Airport & Immigration

Arriving at Incheon International Airport (ICN) — the most common entry point to Korea

🛂 At Immigration (입국심사)
Officer
방문 목적이 무엇인가요?
Bang-mun mok-jeok-i mu-eo-sin-ga-yo?
What is the purpose of your visit?
You
관광입니다.
Gwan-gwang-im-ni-da.
I'm here for tourism.
Officer
얼마나 머무실 예정인가요?
Eol-ma-na meo-mu-sil ye-jeong-in-ga-yo?
How long are you planning to stay?
You
2주 동안 머물 예정입니다.
I-ju dong-an meo-mul ye-jeong-im-ni-da.
I'm planning to stay for two weeks.
Officer
숙소 주소가 어떻게 되나요?
Suk-so ju-so-ga eo-tteo-ke doe-na-yo?
What is your accommodation address?
You
서울 명동 호텔에 묵을 예정입니다.
Seo-ul Myeong-dong ho-tel-e mu-gul ye-jeong-im-ni-da.
I'll be staying at a hotel in Myeongdong, Seoul.
💡 Tip: Immigration officers at Incheon usually speak some English, but knowing these phrases shows respect and speeds things up. Have your return ticket and hotel booking ready to show.
🧳 At Customs (세관)
Officer
신고할 물건이 있으신가요?
Sin-go-hal mul-geon-i i-sseu-sin-ga-yo?
Do you have anything to declare?
You
없습니다.
Eop-seum-ni-da.
Nothing to declare.

Hotel Check-in & Requests

Everything you need from arrival to checkout

🔑 Check-in (체크인)
You
체크인 하고 싶은데요.
Che-keu-in ha-go si-peun-de-yo.
I'd like to check in.
Staff
예약 성함이 어떻게 되세요?
Ye-yak seong-ham-i eo-tteo-ke doe-se-yo?
What name is the reservation under?
You
제 이름으로 예약했어요. [이름]입니다.
Je i-reum-eu-ro ye-yak-haess-eo-yo. [name]-im-ni-da.
I made a reservation under my name. It's [name].
Staff
여권 보여주시겠어요?
Yeo-gwon bo-yeo-ju-si-gess-eo-yo?
Could you show me your passport?
🛎️ Requesting Items (프론트 요청)
You
수건 좀 더 가져다 주시겠어요?
Su-geon jom deo ga-jyeo-da ju-si-gess-eo-yo?
Could you bring more towels?
You
와이파이 비밀번호가 뭐예요?
Wa-i-pa-i bi-mil-beon-ho-ga mwo-ye-yo?
What's the Wi-Fi password?
You
에어컨 작동이 안 돼요.
E-eo-kon jak-dong-i an dwae-yo.
The air conditioning isn't working.
You
체크아웃은 몇 시까지예요?
Che-keu-a-ut-eun myeot si-kka-ji-ye-yo?
What time is checkout?
💡 Korean Hotel Tip: Most Korean hotels have a key card that controls the room's electricity — insert it into the slot by the door when you enter. Also, room slippers are provided and it's normal to wear them inside.

Restaurant & Ordering Food

Korea's food culture is unique — use these to navigate it like a local

🪑 Getting a Table (자리 잡기)
You
두 명이에요.
Du myeong-i-e-yo.
There are two of us.
You
자리 있어요?
Ja-ri i-sseo-yo?
Do you have a table available?
Staff
이쪽으로 오세요.
I-jjo-geu-ro o-se-yo.
Please come this way.
📋 Ordering (주문하기)
You
저기요!
Jeo-gi-yo!
Excuse me! (calling a server)
You
이거 주세요.
I-geo ju-se-yo.
I'll have this one, please. (pointing at menu)
You
삼겹살 2인분 주세요.
Sam-gyeop-sal i-in-bun ju-se-yo.
Two servings of samgyeopsal, please.
You
맵지 않게 해주세요.
Maep-ji an-ke hae-ju-se-yo.
Please make it not spicy.
You
물 한 잔 주세요.
Mul han jan ju-se-yo.
One glass of water, please.
💳 Paying the Bill (계산하기)
You
계산해 주세요.
Gye-san-hae ju-se-yo.
Check, please. / I'd like to pay.
You
카드 돼요?
Ka-deu dwae-yo?
Can I pay by card?
You
따로따로 계산해 주세요.
Tta-ro-tta-ro gye-san-hae ju-se-yo.
Please bill us separately.
💡 Restaurant Culture: In Korea, you call the server by saying 저기요! (jeo-gi-yo) — it means "Excuse me" and is never rude. Tipping is NOT expected and can be awkward. Water is usually self-service (무료 — free). Many restaurants have a call button on the table.

Transportation

Subway, bus, taxi and KTX — getting around Korea

🚇 Subway (지하철)
You
명동역 어떻게 가요?
Myeong-dong-yeok eo-tteo-ke ga-yo?
How do I get to Myeongdong Station?
You
이 열차가 홍대입구역 가나요?
I yeol-cha-ga Hong-dae-ip-gu-yeok ga-na-yo?
Does this train go to Hongik University Station?
You
어디서 갈아타야 해요?
Eo-di-seo ga-ra-ta-ya hae-yo?
Where do I transfer?
🚕 Taxi (택시)
You
경복궁으로 가주세요.
Gyeong-bok-gung-eu-ro ga-ju-se-yo.
Please take me to Gyeongbokgung Palace.
You
여기서 세워 주세요.
Yeo-gi-seo se-wo ju-se-yo.
Please stop here.
You
얼마예요?
Eol-ma-ye-yo?
How much is it?
You
카드로 낼게요.
Ka-deu-ro nael-ge-yo.
I'll pay by card.
💡 Transport Tips:
• Buy a T-money card (티머니) at any convenience store — works on subway, bus and taxi nationwide. Tap to pay, much cheaper than buying tickets.
• Kakao Taxi app is the best taxi app — shows price estimate, accepts foreign cards, has English mode.
• Subway announcements are in Korean, English, Chinese and Japanese.

Shopping

Myeongdong, Dongdaemun, Olive Young — shop like a local

💰 Asking Prices & Buying (가격 흥정)
You
이거 얼마예요?
I-geo eol-ma-ye-yo?
How much is this?
You
좀 깎아 주세요.
Jom kka-kka ju-se-yo.
Can you give me a discount?
You
이거 살게요.
I-geo sal-ge-yo.
I'll take this.
You
다른 색 있어요?
Da-reun saek i-sseo-yo?
Do you have this in another color?
You
입어봐도 돼요?
I-beo-bwa-do dwae-yo?
Can I try this on?
You
영수증 주세요. 면세 되나요?
Yeong-su-jeung ju-se-yo. Myeon-se doe-na-yo?
Receipt please. Is this tax-free?
💡 Shopping Tips:
• Tax Refund (환급): Foreigners can get VAT (10%) refunded at the airport on purchases over ₩30,000. Look for "Tax Free" signs.
• Bargaining is OK at Dongdaemun and traditional markets, but NOT at Olive Young, department stores, or convenience stores.
• Olive Young (K-beauty) has an English app with member discounts — download before you go.

Asking for Directions

Get back on track when you're lost

📍 Finding Places (길 찾기)
You
죄송한데요, 길 좀 여쭤봐도 될까요?
Joe-song-han-de-yo, gil jom yeo-jwo-bwa-do doel-kka-yo?
Excuse me, may I ask for directions?
You
경복궁이 어디예요?
Gyeong-bok-gung-i eo-di-ye-yo?
Where is Gyeongbokgung Palace?
You
지하철역이 어디예요?
Ji-ha-cheol-yeok-i eo-di-ye-yo?
Where is the subway station?
You
걸어서 얼마나 걸려요?
Geo-reo-seo eol-ma-na geol-lyeo-yo?
How long does it take on foot?
You
길을 잃었어요.
Gi-reul i-reo-sseo-yo.
I'm lost.

Emergency Phrases

Korea is very safe, but know these just in case

🚑 Medical Emergency

살려주세요!
sal-lyeo-ju-se-yo — Help me! / Save me!
구급차를 불러주세요!
gu-geup-cha-reul bul-leo-ju-se-yo — Call an ambulance!
Emergency number: 119 (fire & ambulance) | Police: 112

👮 Police / Lost Items

도둑이야!
do-du-gi-ya — Thief!
지갑을 잃어버렸어요.
ji-ga-beul i-reo-beo-ryeo-sseo-yo — I lost my wallet.
경찰을 불러주세요.
gyeong-cha-reul bul-leo-ju-se-yo — Please call the police.
Lost & Found: ☎ 182 (Korean Lost Property Center)

🏥 Hospital / Pharmacy

병원이 어디예요?
byeong-won-i eo-di-ye-yo — Where is the hospital?
약국이 어디예요?
yak-gug-i eo-di-ye-yo — Where is the pharmacy?
여기가 아파요.
yeo-gi-ga a-pa-yo — It hurts here. (pointing)
Most large hospitals have English-speaking staff. Search "foreigner-friendly hospital Korea" for a list.
💡 Safety in Korea: Korea ranks among the world's safest countries. The streets are well-lit, CCTV is everywhere, and people are generally very helpful to lost tourists. If you need help, ask at any convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven) — staff can often help or call for you.

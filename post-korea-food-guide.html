🍜 Korean Food Guide

From sizzling BBQ to comforting soups, spicy stews to sweet street snacks — your complete guide to eating in Korea.

🍖 Must-Try Korean Dishes

These are the dishes every visitor to Korea should try at least once.

🥩
Samgyeopsal
삼겹살

Grilled pork belly cooked at your table on a charcoal or gas grill. Wrap in lettuce with garlic, green onion and ssamjang paste.

● Not spicy
🍲
Bibimbap
비빔밥

Rice topped with assorted vegetables, a fried egg, and gochujang (red chili paste). Mix everything together before eating.

● Mildly spicy (adjustable)
🥘
Kimchi Jjigae
김치찌개

Fermented kimchi simmered into a rich, deeply flavored stew with pork or tofu. Korea's ultimate comfort food.

● Spicy
🍜
Ramyeon
라면

Korean instant noodles — miles beyond the global version. Best eaten at a pojangmacha (street tent) at 2am.

● Mildly spicy
🐙
Nakji Bokkeum
낙지볶음

Stir-fried spicy octopus — a dish that will clear your sinuses and delight your palate at the same time.

● Very spicy 🔥
🥗
Japchae
잡채

Glass noodles stir-fried with vegetables, beef and sesame oil. A festive dish that's popular at celebrations.

● Not spicy
🍗
Chimaek
치맥

Korean fried chicken + beer (maekju) — a national institution. Crispy, saucy and made for sharing while watching sports.

● Not spicy (original) / spicy versions available
🥣
Sundubu Jjigae
순두부찌개

Soft silken tofu stew with egg, seafood or meat, served bubbling hot in a stone pot.

● Spicy
🍱
Dosirak
도시락

Korean lunch box — rice, protein and side dishes. Available at convenience stores for ₩3,000–5,000. A budget traveler's best friend.

● Varies

🔥 Korean BBQ Guide

Korean BBQ (고기구이, go-gi gu-i) is an experience, not just a meal. Here's how it works:

1

Sit down — banchan arrives automatically

Small side dishes (반찬, ban-chan) are placed on the table for free — kimchi, pickled vegetables, bean sprouts, fish cake. Refills are always free, just ask.

2

Order your meat

Popular choices: 삼겹살 (pork belly), 목살 (pork neck), 소갈비 (beef ribs), 불고기 (marinated beef). Most restaurants require a minimum of 2 servings per type.

3

The server grills (or you grill)

At most mid-range restaurants, the server grills and cuts the meat for you. At cheaper spots, you're on your own — it's easy.

4

Make a ssam wrap

Take a lettuce or perilla leaf, add rice, a piece of meat, garlic, sliced chili and a dab of ssamjang paste. Fold and eat in one bite.

5

End with naengmyeon or doenjang jjigae

Koreans often finish BBQ with cold buckwheat noodles (냉면) or a soybean paste stew (된장찌개). Order separately.

💡 BBQ Tips: Your clothes will smell like smoke — that's normal and unavoidable. Many BBQ restaurants have coat hooks outside for that reason. Most have ventilation hoods above the grill but it still lingers. Plan accordingly.

🏪 Street Food

🍢

Tteokbokki (떡볶이)

Chewy rice cakes in sweet spicy red sauce. Korea's most beloved street food.

🥞

Hotteok (호떡)

Sweet pancake filled with brown sugar, cinnamon and nuts. Perfect winter food.

🍡

Odeng (어묵)

Fish cake skewers in hot broth — warming, savory and cheap (₩500 each).

🌽

Roasted Corn (구운 옥수수)

Charcoal-roasted corn with butter — common at beaches and market stalls.

🍦

Bingsu (빙수)

Shaved ice with sweetened red bean, condensed milk and toppings. Korea's iconic summer dessert.

🐔

Dakgangjeong (닭강정)

Sweet crispy fried chicken bites — addictively good, especially from Myeongdong vendors.

🥬 Vegetarian & Dietary Tips

Eating with Dietary Restrictions

  • Vegetarian: Traditional Korean Buddhist temple food (사찰음식) is fully vegan. Look for 사찰 식당 (temple food restaurants). Bibimbap and japchae can often be made without meat — ask.
  • Vegan: Harder than vegetarian — many broths contain anchovy or beef stock. Say "고기 없이 해주세요" (go-gi eop-si hae-ju-se-yo) — no meat please.
  • Gluten-free: Rice is the staple so many dishes are naturally GF. Soy sauce (간장) contains gluten — ask for gluten-free soy (글루텐프리 간장).
  • Halal: Growing availability in Itaewon (Seoul) and Dongdaemun. Search "Halal restaurant Korea" on Naver Maps.

💰 Budget Guide — How Much Does Food Cost?

Average Meal Prices (2026)

  • Convenience store meal (dosirak + drink): ₩4,000–6,000 (~$3–4)
  • Street food snack (tteokbokki, odeng): ₩2,000–4,000 (~$1.50–3)
  • Local restaurant lunch set: ₩8,000–12,000 (~$6–9)
  • Korean BBQ per person: ₩15,000–25,000 (~$11–18)
  • Upscale restaurant: ₩30,000–60,000+ per person
  • Chimaek (chicken + beer) for 2: ₩30,000–40,000 (~$22–30)

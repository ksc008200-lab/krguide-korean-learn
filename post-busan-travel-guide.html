🏖️ Top Attractions

🌊

Haeundae Beach

Korea's most famous beach — bustling in summer, peaceful in autumn. Backed by luxury hotels and seafood restaurants.

🎨

Gamcheon Culture Village

Busan's most photogenic spot — colorful houses cascading down a hillside, with street art, tiny galleries and cafes tucked in every corner.

🐟

Jagalchi Fish Market

Korea's largest seafood market — pick your live fish, have it prepared right there and eat it fresh with soju and side dishes.

🌉

Gwangan Bridge

A stunning 7.4km suspension bridge — best viewed from Gwangalli Beach at night when it's lit up in colors.

⛩️

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

A rare seaside Buddhist temple built on dramatic coastal cliffs — stunning at sunrise and during the lotus lantern festival.

🏔️

Taejongdae Park

Dramatic coastal cliffs at Busan's southern tip — a lighthouse, rocky coastline, and sweeping views of the Korea Strait.

🍽️ What to Eat in Busan

Busan's food scene is defined by the sea. The city has its own distinct dishes you won't find as easily in Seoul.

🐟

Millak Raw Fish (횟집)

Fresh sashimi-style fish at Millak Waterside Park — the local way to eat seafood.

🦪

Dongnae Pajeon (동래파전)

Busan's famous green onion pancake loaded with seafood — crispy, chewy perfection.

🍢

Oden / Odeng (어묵)

Fish cake on a stick in hot broth — a beloved Busan street food, especially in winter.

🍜

Dwaeji Gukbap (돼지국밥)

Pork and rice soup — Busan's signature comfort food, eaten for breakfast by locals.

🐙

Nakji Bokkeum (낙지볶음)

Spicy stir-fried octopus — a Busan specialty that's fiery and deeply flavorful.

🎂

BIFF Square Hotteok (씨앗호떡)

Sweet pancakes stuffed with seeds, brown sugar and honey — a must at the street stalls.

Where to eat: Jagalchi Market for raw seafood · Gukje Market for street food · BIFF Square for hotteok and snacks · Seomyeon for late-night Korean BBQ

🚆 Getting to Busan from Seoul

🚅

KTX Bullet Train — Recommended

Seoul Station → Busan Station in 2 hours 20 minutes. Trains run every 20–30 minutes from 5am to midnight. Book on Korail or the SRT app. Cost: ~₩59,800 (economy).

✈️

Domestic Flight

Gimpo (Seoul) → Gimhae (Busan). ~1 hour flight but factoring in airport time, KTX is usually faster and cheaper.

🚌

Express Bus

Seoul Express Bus Terminal → Busan Central Bus Terminal. ~4.5 hours. Cheapest option (~₩23,000) but slower.

🗺️ Getting Around Busan

Local Transport

  • Metro (지하철) — 4 lines covering most attractions. T-money card works.
  • Bus — Extensive network, but harder for tourists to navigate. T-money card works.
  • Taxi — Very affordable. KakaoTaxi app recommended. Base fare: ~₩4,800.
  • Busan City Tour Bus — Hop-on hop-off covering all major sights. Great for first-timers.

📅 Best Time to Visit

By Season

  • Spring (Mar–May) — Cherry blossoms at Nakdonggang River, warm and uncrowded
  • Summer (Jun–Aug) — Beach season, very hot and crowded, Haeundae Sand Festival in July
  • Autumn (Sep–Nov) — Best weather, foliage at Geumjeongsan, Busan International Film Festival (October)
  • Winter (Dec–Feb) — Quietest and cheapest, good for seafood, fewer tourists

🚇 Korea Transportation Guide

Korea has world-class public transport — once you understand the system, getting around is easy, cheap and fast.

💳 T-money Card — Start Here

The T-money card (티머니 카드) is a rechargeable transit card that works nationwide on all subways, city buses, and most taxis. It's the single most important thing to get on arrival.

Where to buy

  • Any convenience store: GS25, CU, 7-Eleven, Emart24
  • Incheon Airport arrival hall (after customs)
  • Subway station vending machines
  • Cost: ₩3,000–5,000 (card deposit included)

Benefits

  • Cheaper than single-journey tickets
  • Transfer discounts between subway and bus
  • Works in all major Korean cities
  • Top up at any convenience store or subway machine
💡 Tip: Load at least ₩30,000–50,000 when you arrive. Minimum balance for a subway ride is around ₩1,400. If your card runs out mid-journey, tap at an add-fare machine (잔액보충기) before exiting.

🛬 From Incheon Airport to Seoul

🚂
AREX — Airport Railroad Express Best Value

The most popular option. Two services run from Incheon Airport Terminal 1 & 2:

  • Direct (직통): Incheon → Seoul Station in 43 minutes. ₩11,000. No stops.
  • All-stop (일반): Incheon → Seoul Station in 66 minutes. ₩4,850 (T-money). Stops at Gimpo, Hongdae, etc.
Direct (no stops)₩11,000 (~$8)
All-stop (T-money)₩4,850 (~$3.50)
🚌
Airport Limousine Bus Comfortable

Premium buses that go directly to major hotels and neighborhoods in Seoul. Good if your hotel is on the route.

  • Takes 60–90 minutes depending on traffic
  • Cost: ₩17,000–20,000 depending on route
  • Routes shown on boards at arrival hall — look for your destination
🚕
Taxi / KakaoTaxi

Convenient but expensive from the airport — heavy traffic on the highway and a toll road fee on top.

  • Regular taxi: ₩60,000–90,000 to central Seoul
  • Kakao Black (premium): ₩80,000–120,000
  • Use only metered taxis from official taxi stands — avoid touts

🚇 Seoul Subway

Seoul's subway is one of the world's best — 9 major lines, 300+ stations, signage in Korean/English/Chinese/Japanese, and trains every 2–5 minutes.

Line 1 (1호선)Seoul Station, Dongdaemun, Incheon
Line 2 (2호선)Gangnam, Hongdae, Sinchon, City Hall
Line 3 (3호선)Gyeongbokgung, Express Bus Terminal
Line 4 (4호선)Myeongdong, Dongdaemun History Park
Line 5 (5호선)Gimpo Airport, Yeouido, Gwanghwamun
Line 6 (6호선)Itaewon, Mapo, DMC
Line 7 (7호선)Gangnam, Nowon, Dobongsan
Line 9 (9호선)Gimpo Airport → Gangnam (express)

Subway Tips

  • Download Naver Maps or KakaoMap for real-time subway routes — more accurate than Google Maps in Korea
  • Stand on the right side of escalators — left side is for walking
  • Priority seating (노약자석) is for elderly, pregnant women, and disabled — don't sit there even if it's empty
  • Eating on the subway is uncommon and frowned upon — finish your coffee before boarding
  • Subway runs approximately 5:30am–midnight. Last trains vary by line.

🚅 KTX Bullet Train — City to City

The Korea Train Express (KTX) connects major cities at speeds up to 305 km/h. The most comfortable and reliable way to travel between Seoul, Busan, Daegu, Daejeon and Gwangju.

💡 KTX Booking: Book on Korail (letskorail.com) or the Korail Talk app. Foreign credit cards work. Book at least 3–7 days ahead for popular weekend trains — they sell out. Window seats fill up first.

🚕 Taxis

📱
Kakao Taxi (카카오택시)

Korea's dominant taxi app. English available, shows fare estimate before booking, accepts foreign cards. Download before you arrive. Kakao T app — the same as KakaoTalk but transport-focused.

Taxi Types & Fares (Seoul)

  • Regular (일반): Base fare ₩4,800. Orange or silver cars. Available everywhere.
  • Large (모범): Premium black vehicles, higher fare (~1.5x), professional drivers. Good for airport runs.
  • Kakao Black: Premium, English-speaking driver available, flat-rate option. Best for big groups or late nights.
  • Night surcharge: Fares increase by 20% between midnight and 4am.

🚌 Intercity Bus

Express buses (고속버스) are the cheapest way to travel between cities. Slightly slower than KTX but serve many cities the train doesn't reach.

Key Bus Terminals in Seoul

  • Seoul Express Bus Terminal (강남) — buses to Busan, Daegu, Gwangju, Jeonju
  • Dong Seoul Bus Terminal (건대입구) — buses to east coast cities, Gangneung, Sokcho
  • Book online at kobus.co.kr or at the terminal
  • Prices roughly 40–50% cheaper than KTX for same routes

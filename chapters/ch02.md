*Chapter 2 · 제2장*
# Korean Consonants
**한국어 자음 — 소리의 기초**

## What is a Consonant? · 자음이란 무엇인가?
Consonants are sounds produced by partially or completely blocking the flow of air through the mouth. Korean has **14 basic consonants** (기본 자음) and **5 tense consonants** (쌍자음). Their shapes were scientifically designed to mirror the physical position of the mouth and tongue when producing each sound — a design feature unique to Hangul in world writing systems.

자음은 입을 통한 공기 흐름을 방해하여 만들어지는 소리입니다. 한국어에는 **14개 기본 자음**과 **5개 쌍자음**이 있으며, 각 글자 모양은 그 소리를 낼 때 입과 혀의 실제 위치를 표현하도록 과학적으로 설계되었습니다. 이는 세계 문자 중 한글만의 독특한 특징입니다.

### — The 5 Root Positions · 5개의 기본 위치 —

| Position | Hangul Root | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 어금닛소리 (velar) | ㄱ | Back of tongue raised |
| 혓소리 (alveolar) | ㄴ | Tongue tip at upper teeth |
| 입술소리 (labial) | ㅁ | Both lips together |
| 잇소리 (sibilant) | ㅅ | Air through narrow gap |
| 목구멍소리 (glottal) | ㅇ | Throat / no obstruction |

## The 14 Basic Consonants · 14개의 기본 자음

| Hangul | Name | Romanization | Sound | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ㄱ | 기역 (gi-yeok) | g / k | g in "go" | 가다 (ga-da) — to go |
| ㄴ | 니은 (ni-eun) | n | n in "name" | 나 (na) — I |
| ㄷ | 디귿 (di-geut) | d / t | d in "day" | 다 (da) — all |
| ㄹ | 리을 (ri-eul) | r / l | between r and l | 라 (ra) |
| ㅁ | 미음 (mi-eum) | m | m in "mom" | 마 (ma) |
| ㅂ | 비읍 (bi-eup) | b / p | b in "boy" | 바 (ba) |
| ㅅ | 시옷 (si-ot) | s | s in "see" | 사 (sa) |
| ㅇ | 이응 (i-eung) | silent / ng | silent at start, ng at end | 아 (a) |
| ㅈ | 지읒 (ji-eut) | j | j in "joy" | 자 (ja) |
| ㅊ | 치읓 (chi-eut) | ch | ch in "chair" | 차 (cha) |
| ㅋ | 키읔 (ki-euk) | k (aspirated) | k in "kite" | 카 (ka) |
| ㅌ | 티읕 (ti-eut) | t (aspirated) | t in "top" | 타 (ta) |
| ㅍ | 피읖 (pi-eup) | p (aspirated) | p in "park" | 파 (pa) |
| ㅎ | 히읗 (hi-eut) | h | h in "hat" | 하 (ha) |

## The 5 Tense Consonants · 5개의 쌍자음

Tense consonants are produced with extra tension in the vocal cords — sharper, more forceful than plain consonants.

쌍자음은 성대에 긴장을 주어 더 강하고 날카롭게 발음합니다.

| Hangul | Name | Romanization | How to Pronounce |
|---|---|---|---|
| ㄲ | 쌍기역 | kk | Tense ㄱ — like saying "skill" but harder |
| ㄸ | 쌍디귿 | tt | Tense ㄷ — like "stop" but harder |
| ㅃ | 쌍비읍 | pp | Tense ㅂ — clipped, sharp |
| ㅆ | 쌍시옷 | ss | Tense ㅅ — hissing, longer |
| ㅉ | 쌍지읒 | jj | Tense ㅈ — sharp j sound |

### Minimal Pairs to Hear the Difference · 최소 대립쌍

| Plain | Tense | Aspirated | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| 가다 (ga-da) | 까다 (kka-da) | 카다 (ka-da) | go / peel / (no meaning) |
| 달 (dal) | 딸 (ttal) | 탈 (tal) | moon / daughter / mask |
| 불 (bul) | 뿔 (ppul) | 풀 (pul) | fire / horn / grass |
| 사다 (sa-da) | 싸다 (ssa-da) | (none) | buy / cheap |
| 자다 (ja-da) | 짜다 (jja-da) | 차다 (cha-da) | sleep / salty / cold |

> 💡 **Tip:** Native English speakers often confuse these three sounds. Listening exercises with minimal pairs (like above) are the fastest way to train your ear.

## Aspirated vs Tense vs Plain · 거센소리 vs 된소리 vs 예사소리

Korean consonants come in three flavors. Master this trio early.

| Plain | Aspirated (거센소리) | Tense (된소리) |
|---|---|---|
| ㄱ (g/k) | ㅋ (k, breathy) | ㄲ (kk, sharp) |
| ㄷ (d/t) | ㅌ (t, breathy) | ㄸ (tt, sharp) |
| ㅂ (b/p) | ㅍ (p, breathy) | ㅃ (pp, sharp) |
| ㅅ (s) | (none) | ㅆ (ss, hissed) |
| ㅈ (j) | ㅊ (ch, breathy) | ㅉ (jj, sharp) |

## How to Practice · 발음 연습법

> 🎯 **Hand-on-mouth drill**
>
> 1. Put your palm in front of your mouth.
> 2. Say ㅍ (pa) — you should feel a strong puff of breath. ✅ aspirated.
> 3. Say ㅃ (ppa) — almost no breath. ✅ tense.
> 4. Say ㅂ (ba) — moderate puff. ✅ plain.
>
> Once you can FEEL the difference, you can HEAR the difference.

💡 **Tip:** To make the Korean ㄹ sound, quickly flap your tongue against the roof of your mouth — like the "tt" sound in the American English word "butter" or "water." That's very close to ㄹ!

한국어 ㄹ 소리를 내려면, 혀를 입천장에 빠르게 튕기세요 — 미국 영어의 "butter"나 "water"의 "tt" 소리와 매우 비슷합니다!

## Order in the Dictionary · 사전 순서

When you look up a Korean word, consonants are ordered like this:

ㄱ → ㄲ → ㄴ → ㄷ → ㄸ → ㄹ → ㅁ → ㅂ → ㅃ → ㅅ → ㅆ → ㅇ → ㅈ → ㅉ → ㅊ → ㅋ → ㅌ → ㅍ → ㅎ

> 💡 **Memory chunk:** 14 base + 5 tense (쌍-) = 19 total consonants. Tense consonants come right after their plain counterparts.

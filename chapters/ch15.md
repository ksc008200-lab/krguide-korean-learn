## Side Dishes (반찬) Culture · 반찬 문화

Korean meals come with multiple 반찬 (side dishes) — usually free and refillable. The standard accompaniments:

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 김치 | kimchi (fermented cabbage) |
| 깍두기 | radish kimchi |
| 콩나물 | seasoned bean sprouts |
| 시금치나물 | seasoned spinach |
| 멸치볶음 | stir-fried anchovies |
| 계란말이 | rolled omelette |
| 단무지 | yellow pickled radish |

> 💡 You can always ask "반찬 더 주실 수 있어요?" — it's expected and free in most Korean restaurants.

## Korean Eating Etiquette · 한국 식사 예절

- Wait for the eldest to start first
- Don't lift your rice bowl off the table (Japanese style is wrong here)
- Pour drinks for others, never yourself
- Use both hands when receiving food/drinks from elders
- Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral imagery)
- Slurping noodles is OK; eating loudly with mouth open is rude

## Top 10 Must-Try Korean Foods for Foreigners · 외국인이 꼭 먹어봐야 할 한국 음식 TOP 10

1. **삼겹살** — Grilled pork belly with lettuce wraps
2. **비빔밥** — Mixed rice (Jeonju version is iconic)
3. **김치찌개** — Spicy kimchi stew
4. **치맥** — Korean fried chicken + beer
5. **떡볶이** — Spicy street snack
6. **김밥** — Portable rolls
7. **불고기** — Sweet marinated beef
8. **냉면** — Cold noodles (summer)
9. **순두부찌개** — Soft tofu stew
10. **호떡** — Sweet pancake (winter street food)

> 💡 한국 음식은 단지 먹는 것이 아니라 — 함께 먹는 행위가 핵심. 식당에서 혼자보다 함께 가는 게 진짜 한국식 경험입니다.

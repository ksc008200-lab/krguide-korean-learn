# Love & Dating — Korean Romance Culture!
**사랑과 연애 — 한국식 로맨스 문화!**

사랑 & 연애 · sa-rang & yeon-ae · The Language of the Heart!

💡 **Tip:** Korean romance has its own beautiful culture! From the 100-day anniversary celebration to couple matching outfits, Korean dating culture is unique, sweet, and deeply expressive. K-dramas have shown the world how romantic Koreans can be — now learn the real language behind the love! 💕

## Love & Feelings Vocabulary · 사랑과 감정 단어

> ❤️ **사랑 (sa-rang)** — love. The all-encompassing word.
> 💘 **첫사랑 (cheot sa-rang)** — first love. A sacred memory in Korean culture.
> 🥺 **짝사랑 (jjak sa-rang)** — unrequited love (literally "one-sided love").
> 💑 **연인 (yeon-in)** — couple / lover.
> 💍 **결혼 (gyeol-hon)** — marriage.
> 💔 **이별 (i-byeol)** — breakup / parting.

## Korean Couple Culture · 한국 커플 문화

> 💯 **100일 (100-day anniversary)** — Massive milestone. Couples exchange rings, take photos, post on Instagram.
> 👫 **커플룩 (Couple look)** — Matching outfits, shoes, even phone cases.
> 📅 **Korean dating anniversaries:** 100일, 200일, 300일, 1년, 2년...
> 💝 **Pepero Day (11월 11일)** — Couples exchange long Pepero biscuits.
> 🍫 **발렌타인데이 (Feb 14)** — Women give chocolate to men.
> 🤍 **화이트데이 (Mar 14)** — Men give candy/gifts back to women.
> 🖤 **블랙데이 (Apr 14)** — Singles eat 짜장면 together to mourn singleness.

## Cultural Notes · 문화 노트

> 💖 **연애 ≠ 결혼** — Dating doesn't automatically lead to marriage. Many Korean couples date 2-5 years before considering marriage.

> 👰 **부모님 인사** — Meeting the parents is a serious step, usually after a year. It signals marriage intention.

> 💔 **솔로 vs 모태솔로** — "솔로" = single. "모태솔로" = single since birth (never had a relationship). A common K-drama trope.

> 💡 Korean romantic culture is rich, sweet, and intense. Learning these phrases gives you the keys to one of the most beloved aspects of Korean media and life.

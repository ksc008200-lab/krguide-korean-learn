## The Two Great Traditional Holidays · 2대 명절

> 🌕 **설날 (Seol-nal) — Lunar New Year**

Korea's most important holiday. Families travel home, perform 차례 ancestral rites, children bow (세배) to elders and receive 세뱃돈 (New Year money). Everyone eats 떡국 (rice cake soup) — eating it traditionally adds one year to your age!

한국 최대 명절. 가족이 고향에 모이고, 차례를 지내며, 아이들은 어른에게 세배하고 세뱃돈을 받습니다. 모두 떡국을 먹는데 — 전통적으로 떡국을 먹으면 한 살을 더 먹는다고 합니다!

> 🌾 **추석 (Chu-seok) — Korean Thanksgiving**

The harvest moon festival on lunar August 15. Families visit ancestral graves (성묘), make 송편 (half-moon rice cakes), and gather under the full moon. Often called "Korea's Thanksgiving."

음력 8월 15일 추수 명절. 가족이 성묘를 가고, 송편을 빚고, 보름달 아래 모입니다. '한국의 추수감사절'로 불립니다.

## Modern Korean Celebrations · 현대 한국 기념일

> 💕 **발렌타인데이 (Feb 14)** — Women give chocolate to men
> 🤍 **화이트데이 (Mar 14)** — Men give candy to women
> 🖤 **블랙데이 (Apr 14)** — Singles eat 짜장면 (black noodles) together
> 🍫 **빼빼로 데이 (Nov 11)** — Friends exchange Pepero biscuits
> 🎂 **어버이날 (May 8)** — Parents' Day, give carnations and 용돈

## Cultural Insights · 문화 인사이트

> 🎁 **Gift-giving etiquette** — Use both hands when giving and receiving. Cash gifts go in clean envelopes (특히 흰 봉투).
> 🥢 **Eating together** — Wait for the eldest to start first. Pour drinks for others, never yourself.
> 👴 **Family hierarchy** — Holidays reinforce 효 (filial piety). The eldest male typically leads ceremonies.

> 💡 Knowing Korean holidays is more than vocabulary — it's understanding what holds families together for centuries.

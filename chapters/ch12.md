# Grammar Patterns
**문법 패턴 — 진짜 한국어 회화의 열쇠**

문법 패턴 · mun-beop pae-teun · Unlock Real Conversation!

💡 **Tip:** Korean grammar patterns are power-ups! Once you learn a pattern, you can plug in ANY verb and create hundreds of new sentences instantly. This chapter teaches you the 8 most essential patterns that real Koreans use every single day.

## Pattern 1 · ~고 싶어요 · "I want to ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + 고 싶어요
> -go sip-eo-yo = want to ___

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 먹고 싶어요 | I want to eat |
| 가고 싶어요 | I want to go |
| 자고 싶어요 | I want to sleep |
| 사고 싶어요 | I want to buy |
| 배우고 싶어요 | I want to learn |

> ⭐ **Special note**
> 보고 싶어요 literally means "I want to see" — but said to a person, it means **"I miss you."** Romantic! 💕

> 🎯 **Dialogue example**
> A: 오늘 뭐 먹고 싶어요? (What do you want to eat today?)
> B: 저는 삼겹살 먹고 싶어요! (I want to eat samgyeopsal!)
> A: 저도요! 같이 가요! (Me too! Let's go together!)

## Pattern 2 · ~(으)ㄹ 수 있어요 · "I can ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 수 있어요 = can
> Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 수 없어요 = cannot
> Stem ends in vowel → ㄹ 수. Stem ends in consonant → 을 수.

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 수영할 수 있어요 | I can swim |
| 요리할 수 있어요 | I can cook |
| 기타 칠 수 있어요 | I can play guitar |
| 운전할 수 있어요 | I can drive |
| 한국어 할 수 있어요 | I can speak Korean |
| 매운 거 못 먹을 수 없어요 | I cannot eat spicy food |

> 💡 **Variation: -(으)ㄹ 줄 알아요 / 몰라요**
> 자전거 탈 줄 알아요 (I know how to ride a bike) — emphasizes acquired skill, not just ability.

## Pattern 3 · ~아/어야 해요 · "I have to ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -아/어야 해요 (also -야 돼요)

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 공부해야 해요 | I have to study |
| 가야 해요 | I have to go |
| 일어나야 돼요 | I have to wake up |
| 약 먹어야 돼요 | I have to take medicine |
| 빨리 가야 해요 | I have to hurry |

> ⭐ 해요 = neutral. 돼요 = a bit more casual / softer.

## Pattern 4 · ~지 마세요 · "Don't ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -지 마세요 (polite negative command)

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 가지 마세요 | Don't go |
| 먹지 마세요 | Don't eat |
| 걱정하지 마세요 | Don't worry |
| 울지 마세요 | Don't cry |
| 만지지 마세요 | Don't touch |

> 🎯 K-drama line you've heard 1,000 times: **가지 마!** (Don't go!) — casual form.

## Pattern 5 · ~(으)면 · "If / when ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -(으)면

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 비가 오면 | if it rains |
| 시간 있으면 | if you have time |
| 한국에 가면 | when I go to Korea |
| 배고프면 | if I'm hungry |
| 내일 만나면 | when we meet tomorrow |

> 💡 **Common compound:** -(으)면 좋겠어요 = "It would be nice if ___" / "I wish ___"
> 비가 안 왔으면 좋겠어요 = I wish it wouldn't rain.

## Pattern 6 · ~아/어 주세요 · "Please ___ for me"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -아/어 주세요

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 도와주세요 | Please help me |
| 가르쳐 주세요 | Please teach me |
| 천천히 말해 주세요 | Please speak slowly |
| 사진 찍어 주세요 | Please take a picture |
| 이거 보여 주세요 | Please show me this |

> ⭐ **Politeness escalator**
> 주세요 → 주시겠어요? → 주실 수 있을까요? (each one more polite)

## Pattern 7 · ~(으)러 가요 · "I'm going to ___" (purpose)

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -(으)러 + verb of motion (가다 / 오다)

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 밥 먹으러 가요 | I'm going to eat |
| 친구 만나러 가요 | I'm going to meet a friend |
| 영화 보러 가요 | I'm going to watch a movie |
| 공부하러 도서관에 가요 | I'm going to the library to study |
| 운동하러 헬스장에 가요 | I'm going to the gym to work out |

## Pattern 8 · ~(으)니까 · "Because ___"

> 🔧 **Formula**
> Verb stem + -(으)니까 (reason, often used with commands or invitations)

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 비 오니까 우산 가져가세요 | It's raining, so take an umbrella |
| 피곤하니까 일찍 자요 | I'm tired, so let's sleep early |
| 늦었으니까 빨리 가요 | We're late, so hurry |
| 맛있으니까 더 먹어요 | It's tasty, so eat more |
| 시간 없으니까 택시 타요 | We have no time, so let's take a taxi |

> 💡 **Compare with -아/어서**
> -니까 = reason for **command/suggestion**: 비 오니까 우산 가져가세요.
> -아/어서 = reason for **state/result**: 비가 와서 우산을 가져왔어요.

## Combining Patterns · 패턴 조합하기

Real fluency comes from chaining patterns. Examples:

| Korean | English |
|---|---|
| 한국에 가고 싶어서 한국어 배워요 | I'm learning Korean because I want to go to Korea |
| 시간이 있으면 영화 보러 가요 | If I have time, I go to watch a movie |
| 도와줘서 고마워요 | Thanks for helping me |
| 늦었으니까 빨리 가야 돼요 | We're late so we have to hurry |
| 운동할 수 있으면 매일 해요 | If I can exercise, I do it every day |

## How to Master Patterns · 패턴 마스터 방법

> 🎯 **3-step plug-and-play method**
> 1. Memorize the pattern frame (e.g., -고 싶어요).
> 2. Plug in 10 different verbs you already know.
> 3. Speak each version aloud 3 times.
>
> Within a week you'll deploy patterns automatically in real conversation.

> 💡 Memorize these 8 patterns + your existing vocabulary, and you can express 80% of daily Korean conversation. Grammar patterns are the multiplication tables of Korean — everything else builds on them.

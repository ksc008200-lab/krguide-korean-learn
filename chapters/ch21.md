# Adjectives — Describe Everything in Korean!
**형용사 — 한국어로 모든 것을 묘사하기!**

형용사 · hyeong-yong-sa · Color Your Korean with Description!

💡 **Tip:** Korean adjectives are actually verbs! They conjugate. You say 예뻐요 (it's pretty) the same way you say 가요 (I go). This makes Korean adjectives flexible and powerful. 🎨

## How Korean Adjectives Work · 형용사의 규칙

> 📌 **As a Predicate (after noun)** — most common!
> Subject + Adjective + 요
>
> | Korean | English |
> |---|---|
> | 저 사람은 예뻐요 | That person is pretty |
> | 한국어는 재미있어요 | Korean is interesting |
> | 음식이 맛있어요 | The food is delicious |

> 📌 **Before a Noun (modifier form)**
> Adjective stem + ㄴ/은 + Noun
>
> | Korean | English |
> |---|---|
> | 예쁜 사람 | a pretty person |
> | 재미있는 책 | an interesting book |
> | 맛있는 음식 | delicious food |

## Modifier Form Rules · 수식형 규칙

| Stem ending | Add | Example |
|---|---|---|
| vowel | -ㄴ | 예쁘 + ㄴ → 예쁜 (pretty) |
| consonant | -은 | 작 + 은 → 작은 (small) |
| -있다/-없다 | -는 | 재미있다 → 재미있는 (interesting) |

## Adjective Conjugation Quick Table · 형용사 활용 빠른 참고

> 형용사: 예쁘다 (pretty)

| Form | Korean | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| Present (polite) | 예뻐요 | it is pretty |
| Past | 예뻤어요 | it was pretty |
| Future | 예쁠 거예요 | it will be pretty |
| Modifier | 예쁜 (+ noun) | pretty (something) |
| Negation | 안 예뻐요 | it's not pretty |

|---|---|

## Cultural Note · 문화 노트

> 🎯 Compliments in Korean

Direct compliments can feel awkward. Koreans often soften them:
- "예뻐요" — straightforward, polite
- "예쁘시네요" — adds 시 honorific + 네요 (mild surprise)
- "정말 예쁘세요" — emphasizes with 정말

Among friends, casual compliments are common: 너무 귀여워!

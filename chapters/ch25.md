## Korean Education Culture · 한국 교육 문화

> 🎯 **수능 (Suneung) — National College Exam**
> Korea's college entrance exam. Held one day in November. The entire country goes silent during the listening section — airplanes are even rerouted around exam centers.
>
> 🎯 **학원 (Hagwon) — Private Academy**
> After-school cram schools where Korean students go for extra study. Many students attend hagwons until 10 PM or later.
>
> 🎯 **재수 (Jaesu) — Repeat Exam Year**
> Students who miss their target university often take a gap year to retake suneung. There's even 삼수 (3rd attempt) and 사수 (4th).
>
> 🎯 **회식 (Hoesik) — Company Dinner**
> Korean offices have mandatory after-work dinners involving heavy drinking. Refusing is socially difficult.

## Korean Work Culture Snapshot · 한국 직장 문화

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| 야근 (overtime) | Common; often unpaid for salaried workers |
| 회식 culture | Drinking dinners build team bonds (and pressure) |
| 호칭 culture | Address everyone by title + 님 |
| 빨리빨리 | "Hurry hurry" — fast-paced execution |
| 정시 퇴근 | "Leaving on time" — increasingly accepted but still uncommon |
| 칼퇴 | Slang: leaving sharp at clock-out (good thing) |

> 💡 Korean workplace and school culture are intensely social. Knowing the hierarchy vocabulary AND the unwritten 눈치 rules is what separates surviving from thriving.
